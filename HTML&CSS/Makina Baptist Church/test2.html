History

Since it's founding in 1987 Makina Missionary Baptist has passed through the hands of many brothers and sisters untill where it is by now

It had several people who founded it and here is a list of the most renown

  • Makina Baptist Church began in 1987 as a common cell-group by Missionary Lynn Raburn and Pastor James Muhanji along Kabarnet road.

    It later shifted to Kibera,Makina near Kibera law courts in 1988.

    Unfortunately the Kibera Law Courts wanted to expound on its space and the church had already grown therefore it was allocated its current location in Kichinjio Area (it was called so because there was a cattle slaughter house by then) by then Chief Kosgei.

  • Missionary Ernie Hopper aided in construction of Makina Missionary Baptist Church that is currently up with the leadership of Pastor Walter Matete who is currently serving at First Landmark Baptist Church,Satellite

    He has also aided in development and continuation of Theology classes for the upcoming priests and also the young priests.

    He is currently the only Missionary sent from The United States to worship with us.

  • Pastor James Muhanji was one among the many servants of God who served with Makina Missionary Baptist in 1987.

    He was the first Pastor to serve with Missionary Lynn Raburn.

    He also aided with the planning of the church's current location after being moved from Makina area.

    The construction of the church at its current location was done in 1989 with the help of Pastor Shivoka and the other church members at that time.

  • Pastor Walter Matete currently serving at First Landmark Baptist Church,Satellite was the pastor who came in during the time of Missionary Ernie Hopper.
